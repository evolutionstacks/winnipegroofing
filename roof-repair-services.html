Winnipeg’s extreme winter conditions often leads to common roofing issues that homeowners should be aware of . These problems include shingle blow-offs during windstorms .

  • Leaks and Ice Dams: Ice dams form when the roof’s temperature fluctuates, causing snow to melt and refreeze at the edges, leading to leaks .
  • Shingle Damage: Heavy winds can lift or tear shingles, exposing the roof to further damage .
  • Moisture & Mold: Moisture damage is a serious issue, especially in older homes with inadequate ventilation.

Ensure Longevity with Regular Roof Maintenance

Regular care is essential to avoiding expensive repairs . Without routine checks , small problems such as ice dams can escalate quickly .

  • Shingle Replacement: Cracked or missing shingles need to be replaced immediately .
  • Gutter Cleaning: Regular gutter cleaning helps protect the roof from ice dams.
  • Roof Inspections: Professional inspections can help you identify hidden damage before it worsens.

Should You Fix It Yourself or Call a Professional?

While do-it-yourself fixes might seem cost-effective , there are serious drawbacks to fixing roofing issues yourself. Inexperienced homeowners can cause more harm than good .

  • There are signs of water damage: Leaks inside the home are clear indications that water is seeping through the roof .
  • Your roof is aging: Roofs over 15 years old often may need a full replacement.
  • Severe weather: After a major storm , it’s recommended to have a professional assess your roof for hidden damage.

Best Practices for Hiring Roofing Contractors

When hiring a roofer , consider customer reviews. A reputable roofing company should provide transparent pricing . Take time to compare to ensure you're choosing the right team for your roofing project.

  • Check reviews and ratings: Look at online reviews .
  • Verify licenses and insurance: Make sure the company is insured .
  • Ask about warranties: Always get a warranty in writing.
