Common Roofing Issues in Winnipeg

Winnipeg’s heavy snowfall and rain often leads to frequent roof problems that can quickly worsen if left unattended. These issues include damage from heavy snow loads.

  • Leaks and Ice Dams: Ice dams form when the roof’s temperature fluctuates, causing snow to melt and refreeze at the edges, leading to leaks .
  • Shingle Damage: Broken or missing shingles are a frequent occurrence, especially after Winnipeg’s notorious windstorms.
  • Moisture & Mold: Moisture buildup from condensation or leaks can lead to mold growth, damaging both the roof structure and your health .

How Routine Maintenance Can Save You Money

Regular roof maintenance is vital to keeping your roof in top condition . Without routine checks , small problems such as missing shingles can lead to significant damage .

  • Shingle Replacement: Cracked or missing shingles need to be replaced immediately .
  • Gutter Cleaning: Regular gutter cleaning helps protect the roof from ice dams.
  • Roof Inspections: Professional inspections can help you identify hidden damage before it worsens.

Signs You Need a Professional Roofing Contractor

While do-it-yourself fixes might seem cost-effective , there are certain risks to fixing roofing issues yourself. Inexperienced homeowners can void warranties.

  • There are signs of water damage: Leaks inside the home are clear indications that water is seeping through the roof .
  • Your roof is aging: Roofs over 15 years old often require more frequent repairs .
  • Severe weather: After experiencing strong winds, it’s important to have a professional assess your roof for hidden damage.

How to Choose a Roofing Contractor in Winnipeg

When selecting a contractor, consider experience . A reputable roofing company should provide quality guarantees. Research thoroughly to ensure you're choosing the right team for your roofing project.

  • Check reviews and ratings: Look at feedback from previous clients.
  • Verify licenses and insurance: Make sure the company is bonded.
  • Ask about warranties: Always get a warranty in writing.
